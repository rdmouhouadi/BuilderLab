# Code of Conduct for BuilderLab

## Our Pledge
In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in BuilderLab a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, religion, or technology choices.

We commit to treating everyone with respect and consideration, valuing a diversity of ideas and opinions, and helping create a safe environment for collaboration and learning.

## Our Standards
Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Public or private insults or attacks
- Trolling, offensive comments, or personal attacks
- Disruptive behavior that impedes others from participating

## Enforcement
Community members are expected to follow this Code of Conduct. If someone engages in unacceptable behavior, members should report it to the maintainers at rd.mouhouadi@gmail.com .

All reports will be reviewed and acted upon at the discretion of the project maintainers. Consequences may include:

- Warning
- Temporary or permanent removal from the community or repository
- Reporting to relevant authorities if necessary

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by BuilderLab, including but not limited to:

- GitHub/GitLab repositories
- Forums, chat channels (Discord, Slack, etc.)
- Events, hackathons, and any other community activities
